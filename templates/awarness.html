Understanding Sleep Disorders

Sleep plays a vital role in maintaining our mental, physical, and emotional health. Lack of quality sleep can lead to chronic issues like Insomnia, Hypersomnia, and Sleep Apnea. Recognizing their symptoms and adopting preventive methods can greatly improve overall well-being.

Definition: Difficulty falling asleep or staying asleep, leading to poor-quality rest.

Causes

  • Stress, anxiety, or depression
  • Irregular sleep schedules
  • Excessive caffeine or screen use before bed

Prevention Methods

  • Maintain consistent sleep timing
  • Practice relaxation techniques like meditation
  • Avoid stimulants and blue light before bedtime

Definition: Feeling excessively sleepy during the day, even after long hours of sleep at night.

Causes

  • Depression or low physical activity
  • Medication side effects
  • Sleep deprivation or irregular routines

Prevention Methods

  • Engage in daily physical activity
  • Follow a structured sleep-wake schedule
  • Avoid alcohol and sedatives

Definition: A disorder in which breathing repeatedly stops and starts during sleep, leading to fatigue and snoring.

Causes

  • Obesity or excess fat around the neck
  • Nasal congestion or airway blockage
  • Genetic predisposition

Prevention Methods

  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Avoid sleeping on your back
  • Limit alcohol and smoking
